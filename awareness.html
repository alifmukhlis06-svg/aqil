Myths vs Facts

MYTH: Domestic violence only happens in poor, uneducated families.

FACT: Domestic violence affects people of all socioeconomic backgrounds, education levels, and ethnicities.

MYTH: Victims can just leave if they want to.

FACT: Leaving is often the most dangerous time. Victims face barriers like financial dependence, fear for children's safety, and lack of support.

MYTH: Domestic violence is a private family matter.

FACT: Domestic violence is a crime and a public health issue that affects entire communities.

How You Can Help

Listen & Believe

If someone confides in you:

  • Listen without judgment
  • Believe their story
  • Don't blame or criticize
  • Respect their decisions

Offer Support

Practical ways to help:

  • Help create a safety plan
  • Offer temporary shelter
  • Accompany to appointments
  • Document incidents safely

Spread Awareness

Be an advocate:

  • Share educational resources
  • Challenge harmful stereotypes
  • Support local organizations
  • Volunteer your time

Donate
