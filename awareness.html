Awareness & Education

Knowledge is power. Learn the facts, get tips for safety, and discover how you can help.

Important Facts

Fear to Report

Many victims are afraid to report abuse due to fear of retaliation, shame, or not being believed.

Home Incidents

Most domestic violence cases happen at home, making it harder for victims to escape or seek help.

Affects Everyone

Both women and men can be victims of domestic violence, regardless of age, race, or background.

Tips & Solutions

Know Your Rights

Understand the Domestic Violence Act 1994 and your legal protections in Malaysia.

Keep Emergency Contacts

Save important numbers like 999 (Police) and 15999 (Women & Girls Hotline) in your phone.

Save Evidence

Document injuries, keep records of threatening messages, and take photos when safe to do so.

Have a Safety Plan

Plan escape routes, keep important documents ready, and identify safe places to go.

Talk to Trusted People

Confide in family, friends, or professionals who can support you without judgment.

How People Can Help

Listen Without Judging

Provide a safe space for victims to share their experiences without criticism or blame.

Encourage Seeking Help

Gently suggest contacting helplines, shelters, or professional counselors for support.

Share Hotline Numbers

Spread awareness about emergency contacts and support services available in Malaysia.

Report Abuse if Necessary

If someone is in immediate danger, contact authorities (999) to ensure their safety.

Immediate Safety Tips

If you're in danger right now:

  1. Call 999 immediately for police assistance
  2. Try to get to a room with a lock and phone
  3. If you can leave safely, go to a neighbor, friend, or public place
  4. Don't worry about belongings - your safety comes first

If using a shared computer/phone:

  • Clear your browser history after visiting support sites
  • Use private/incognito browsing mode
  • Create a secret email account for support communications
